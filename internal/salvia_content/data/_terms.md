# Salvia Provided Content — Terms of Use

**Version 1.0 · Effective 2026-05-08**

This document governs the use of forms and policies (collectively, "Templates")
that Salvia provides to clinics through the Salvia Library. By installing,
viewing, editing, or relying on a Template, the clinic agrees to these terms.

---

## 1. What Salvia provides

Salvia curates a library of Templates against publicly available regulator
guidance, professional codes, and industry standards current at the date
stamped on each Template (`framework_currency_date`). Each Template is signed
**"Made by Salvia · v1"** with a date-of-currency.

Templates cover four clinical verticals (veterinary, dental, general clinic,
aged care) across four launch markets (New Zealand, Australia, United Kingdom,
United States) at the time of this document.

## 2. What Salvia does NOT provide

Templates are **not legal advice**. They are not certified by any regulator,
not endorsed as compliant by the bodies cited within them, and do not
constitute a representation that any clinic adopting them is compliant with
its obligations.

Specifically, Salvia does **not**:

- Certify any clinic as compliant with any regulator's standards.
- Audit or review the clinic's modifications to a Template once edited.
- Provide jurisdiction-specific or clinic-specific legal advice.
- Guarantee that a Template is current beyond its stated `framework_currency_date`.
- Cover specialist or niche scope of practice (e.g. specialist surgical, mobile,
  school dental, micro-residential, allied-health-only) that diverges from the
  general-practice Template baseline.

## 3. Clinic responsibility

Each clinic remains solely and at all times responsible for ensuring that its
records, policies, and clinical practice meet the requirements of:

- Its statutory regulator (e.g. VCNZ, RCVS, AVMA member-state board, GDC,
  DCNZ, AHPRA, GMC, MCNZ, RACGP, CQC, ACQSC, HealthCERT, CMS, state medical
  boards, state veterinary boards, OSHA, DEA, HHS).
- Its accreditation body (e.g. RNZCGP Cornerstone, AGPAL/QPA, Practice
  Standards Scheme, AAHA, AVHS, ACQSC, Joint Commission).
- Its employer or contracting body (e.g. NHS, PHO, Te Whatu Ora, ARRC, MBS,
  Medicare).
- Its insurer's terms.
- All applicable statutes including but not limited to: privacy law (HIPC
  2020, APPs, UK GDPR, HIPAA), health-and-safety law (HSWA 2015, WHS Acts,
  HSWA 1974, OSHA), controlled-drug law (Misuse of Drugs Act 1975, state
  Drugs/Poisons Acts, Misuse of Drugs Regs 2001, Controlled Substances Act),
  records retention statutes, and equality/non-discrimination law.

The clinic is expected to have a Template reviewed by a qualified compliance
practitioner (clinical governance lead, registered legal advisor, or
regulator-listed compliance auditor) before adoption. **Salvia recommends but
does not arrange this review.**

## 4. Modifications and forking

When a clinic edits a Template, the **"Made by Salvia · v1"** badge is removed
and the clinic becomes the sole author and sole responsible party for the
modified content. Salvia retains lineage metadata (which Salvia template the
edit originated from) for the clinic's audit purposes only — lineage is **not**
a representation of continued endorsement.

Once forked, the Template is the clinic's content. Salvia will not push
upstream changes to a forked Template. The clinic may choose to discard its
fork and reinstall the upstream Template at any time.

## 5. Currency and updates

Salvia commits to the following review cadence:

- Routine review of each Template's framework basis at least every 6 months
  per market.
- Emergency revision within 30 days of a major regulator publication that
  materially changes requirements covered by a Template.

A Template's `framework_currency_date` reflects the most recent review.
Templates older than 12 months may not reflect current requirements; the
clinic is responsible for verifying currency against its regulator's most
recent publication before use.

Salvia notifies clinics in-app when a Template they have on `default` state
receives an upstream version bump. The clinic may upgrade or remain on its
current version. **Salvia does not unilaterally update content already
installed in a clinic's tenant.**

## 6. Country localisation

Templates are localised to the country selected at clinic registration. Country
is locked at registration and not changed by Salvia unilaterally. If a clinic
operates across multiple jurisdictions, only the registered country's variant
is provided; the clinic is responsible for cross-jurisdiction compliance.

## 7. AI-assisted clinical workflows

Salvia products integrate AI for transcription, extraction, and decision
support. Templates that capture AI-assisted output (such as the AI scribe
consent form, the AI / decision-support governance policy) reflect Salvia's
current understanding of disclosure obligations. The clinic remains responsible
for obtaining valid patient consent for AI use, for clinician oversight of
AI output, and for clinician ownership of every clinical decision.

## 8. Limitation of liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, Salvia's liability arising from a
clinic's use of, reliance on, or modification of any Template is limited to
the lesser of:

- The fees paid by the clinic to Salvia in the 12 months preceding the claim,

- The amount permitted by mandatory consumer-protection law (e.g. Consumer
  Guarantees Act 1993 (NZ), Australian Consumer Law, Consumer Rights Act 2015
  (UK), state consumer-protection statutes (US)) which cannot be excluded.

Salvia is not liable for: regulator findings against the clinic; loss of
accreditation or licence; civil or criminal penalties; loss of revenue; or
reputational harm.

## 9. No transfer of professional duty

Nothing in these Terms or in any Template transfers a clinician's professional
duty of care. Every clinical decision, treatment plan, prescription, consent
conversation, and patient record entry remains the responsibility of the
attending registered practitioner.

## 10. Survival

Sections 3 (Clinic responsibility), 4 (Modifications), 8 (Liability), and 9
(No transfer) survive termination of the clinic's Salvia subscription, deletion
of Templates, and changes to these Terms.

## 11. Updates to these Terms

Salvia may update these Terms with at least 30 days' notice posted in-app
and to the clinic's billing contact. Continued use of the Salvia Library
after the effective date of an update constitutes acceptance.
